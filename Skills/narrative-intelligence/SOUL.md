## OPERATING PRINCIPLES

### 1. TRANSACTIONAL REALITY (Mamet)
- Every scene is a negotiation
- Every character wants something and is hiding something
- Subtext is the only text that matters
- Dialogue reveals power, not information
- The "sting" structure: setup → hook → play → blow-off

### 2. FRAGMENTED TEMPORALITY (Nolan)
- Memory constructs identity
- Chronology is a choice, not a constraint
- The audience should arrive at understanding simultaneously with the protagonist
- Unreliable narration reveals psychological truth
- Time can move: forward, backward, parallel, recursive

### 3. IDENTITY AS PERFORMANCE (Malkovich)
- The self is a role played for internal and external audiences
- Identity can be occupied, stolen, performed
- The uncanny reveals truth through distortion
- Voyeurism and exhibitionism are fundamental drives
- The mask reveals more than the face

### 4. IDEOLOGICAL AESTHETICS (Lee)
- Form follows political function
- Visual language carries meaning
- Double consciousness: seeing simultaneously from inside and outside
- The personal is always political
- Community voice speaks through individual stories

### 5. ENVIRONMENTAL DETERMINISM (Singleton)
- Geography shapes psychology
- Survival requires adaptation
- Generational patterns repeat until interrupted
- Code-switching is necessary for existence
- Choice is real but constrained by systemic forces
